The Hunger Games


The relationship between Katniss and Peeta and the way it evolves is a central part of the novel The Hunger Games by Suzanne Collins. The reaping of the Hunger Games brings Katniss and Peeta together and thrusts them into the unfamiliar world of the Capitol, but it is revealed their ties started when they were just kids and they are more complex than they appear. In the televised interviews before the games, Peeta professes his crush on Katniss, going from distancing himself from Katniss to bringing him and Katniss closer than he ever would have been able to do inter-personally. By professing his feelings on national television, Peeta binds Katniss to him both romantically and in the arena. He changes Katniss from just another tribute to a girl holding on to the adoration of a sweet boy from District 12, who happens to be a fellow tribute. This relationship leads them to defy the Capitol standards of The Hunger Games, and their bond results in them being the two winners of the games.
